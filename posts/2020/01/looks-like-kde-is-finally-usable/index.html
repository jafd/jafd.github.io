Looks like KDE is finally usable

Date [ 2020-01-20 ] Tags [ linux kde 2020istheyearoflinuxondesktop ]

Looks like KDE is finally doing the right thing with their 5.18 Plasma desktop.

First, it looks consistent for the first time in years.

Then, its shell is written in something else than Javascript, and it looks like it doesn’t have the mad memory leaks GNOME’s Mutter is famous for.

And then, it’s not bloated, and seems to do the right thing with multi-monitor configurations.

Haven’t yet tried it under Wayland, but I expect it to perform there as well. Unfortunately, the major browser and Electron apps are still ignorant of Wayland, so it’s a no-starter for me. XWayland has quite a few drawbacks when you use HiDPI.

Fonts don’t like drunken in the recent Qt5 anymore, yay!

And I like it so much that KDE guys in Plasma 5.18 fully acknowledge both Gtk2 and Gtk3. GNOME, shove it down your pipe and smoke it!

The global menu is the best thing since sliced bread, and it works flawlessly.

Night color control is there, and it’s native, and has a native configuration dialog, consistent with the rest of KDE. Redshift-gtk was fine, but this is bees knees.

Now, how do I make my config portable between machines…
